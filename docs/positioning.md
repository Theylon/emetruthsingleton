# EmeTruth Positioning

## Core Positioning

- Category: Liquidity infrastructure for prediction markets
- Public role: Specialist market-making partner for onchain prediction markets
- Primary promise: We help prediction markets scale by improving market quality
- Buyer outcome: Better market quality leads to better trader retention

## One-Line Positioning

Primary line:

`We provide the liquidity layer that lets prediction markets scale.`

Operator-facing support line:

`EmeTruth partners with prediction market operators to improve market quality through specialist market making, launch support, and pricing infrastructure.`

## Audience Hierarchy

1. Primary audience: founders and heads of markets at onchain prediction venues
2. Secondary audience: LPs and strategic capital partners
3. Tertiary audience: broader ecosystem observers

## Ideal Customer Profile

- Fast-growing, crypto-native prediction venues
- Best fit: live venues with weak liquidity or weak trader retention
- Secondary fit: near-launch venues that need market design and launch seeding
- Focus: onchain venues
- Geography: global

Poor fit for now:

- Venues that do not value external market-making support
- Venues that cannot support fast operational collaboration around APIs and execution
- Dominant incumbents that are unlikely to pay for specialist support

## Problem Statement

Prediction markets do not scale on access alone. They scale when markets are liquid enough to feel reliable to traders.

When order books are thin and spreads stay wide:

- traders hesitate to participate
- new markets launch weakly
- retention falls
- serious capital stays on the sidelines

For operators, liquidity quality is not a cosmetic problem. It directly affects user experience, market credibility, and platform growth.

## Offer Architecture

Primary offer:

`Full liquidity program for prediction markets`

What the offer includes:

- Ongoing market making for live venues
- Launch seeding for new contracts
- Market design and market-opening advice
- Venue API and execution collaboration where needed
- Pricing support informed by exclusive external data relationships

Commercial posture:

- Retainer-based core relationship
- Rebates or hybrid commercial structures where appropriate
- No public emphasis on investor-return language

## Differentiation

EmeTruth is not positioning as a generalist crypto market maker. The wedge is category-specific.

Differentiators:

- Built specifically for prediction-market microstructure
- Focused on onchain venues rather than broad crypto coverage
- Combines market making with launch and market design support
- Exclusive relationships with external data providers that improve pricing confidence
- Internal workflows that turn fragmented external signals into structured pricing inputs
- Operator-level familiarity with the mechanics and constraints of prediction platforms

Important note:

Avoid naming individual data partners publicly unless there is a specific reason and approval to do so.

## Proof Strategy

Keep proof tightly scoped to defensible claims.

Current usable proof:

- More than $9M in volume routed through the execution engine over one quarter
- Execution infrastructure already used by external traders
- Active platform conversations where the specialization resonates
- Capability to collaborate directly on venue API and launch mechanics

Claims to avoid for now:

- Precise latency numbers that are not measured
- Large-brand comparisons on the public site
- Named counterparties or implied partnerships without clear permission
- Returns, yield, or fund-like performance framing

## Messaging Rules

Use:

- liquidity layer
- market quality
- trader retention
- specialist market making
- prediction market operators
- onchain prediction markets

Avoid or reduce:

- truth discovery
- institutional bedrock
- abstract finance language that does not map to buyer outcomes
- "Capital" as the lead brand signal
- unsupported performance metrics

## Brand Direction

- Preferred brand: EmeTruth
- Avoid leading with: EmeTruth Capital
- Tone: technical, sober, quietly aggressive
- Public impression: infrastructure firm that trades, not a fund that happens to have a website
