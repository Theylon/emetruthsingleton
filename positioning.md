# EmeTruth Positioning

This document summarizes the final positioning decisions made across this session.

It is meant to be the handoff for the next step:

- building talking points
- shaping a sales narrative
- turning the website story into a repeatable pitch

## 1. Core Positioning

### Category

EmeTruth is `liquidity infrastructure for prediction markets`.

### Public role

EmeTruth should be presented as a specialist market-making and liquidity partner for prediction markets, not as a general crypto market maker and not as a fund-first business.

### Core one-line

`EmeTruth makes prediction markets liquid and efficient.`

### Current homepage framing

Hero headline:

`Liquidity Layer that scales Prediction Markets.`

### Main promise

Prediction markets scale when market quality improves.

That means:

- tighter markets
- better execution
- better launches
- stronger trader retention

## 2. Audience Hierarchy

### Primary

Platform operators:

- founders
- heads of markets
- teams running or launching prediction market venues

### Secondary

LPs and strategic capital partners.

They matter, but they should not dominate the public story.

### Positioning rule

The website and sales narrative should always feel platform-first.

LPs should appear as a secondary path that adds credibility and future scale, not as the main business being sold.

## 3. Customer Problem

The core problem is not abstract adoption. It is market quality.

The current framing we aligned on:

- if markets are thin, traders leave
- weak liquidity creates bad user experience
- bad user experience hurts retention
- poor launches are hard to recover from
- serious participation does not stick in weak markets

The product-level insight:

For operators, liquidity is product quality.

## 4. What EmeTruth Offers

The current service architecture is a full liquidity program for prediction markets.

Publicly, that includes:

- ongoing market making
- launch seeding
- market design advice
- venue collaboration

Operationally, the company should be understood as a partner that helps a venue trade better, open better, and retain users more effectively.

The public site should not emphasize pricing structure, retainers, rebates, or deal mechanics.

## 5. Unfair Advantage

The strongest positioning decision from this session:

EmeTruth’s unfair advantage is not just that it makes markets.

It is that it has:

- access to alternative and exclusive data sources
- internal workflows that structure fragmented signals into usable pricing inputs
- execution tuned for prediction markets rather than generic crypto flow

This is the wedge.

### Important messaging rule

Do not use Wintermute or GSR as public comparison copy on the site.

They can be internal reference points for tone and structure, but the public story should stand on EmeTruth’s own edge:

- better inputs
- better workflows
- better execution in this category

## 6. Execution Engine Positioning

The proof section should center on the execution engine.

This was an explicit decision.

### What the engine should communicate

- it exists
- it is already in use
- it supports fast execution
- it stays private until committed
- it has already handled meaningful real-world volume

### Current proof points

- Fast Execution
- Private Until Committed
- `$19M Through The Engine`

### Current proof narrative

The engine is already live, built for fast execution, and keeps activity private until committed. It has already carried real volume in market.

### Important proof rules

Use:

- `$19M` through the engine
- real usage
- private until committed
- fast execution

Avoid:

- fake latency claims
- “battle-tested” boilerplate
- architecture dumping
- vague slogans like `Built, Not Pitched`

## 7. Messaging Rules

### Use more of

- liquidity layer
- market quality
- prediction markets
- trader retention
- better inputs
- execution engine
- private until committed
- in use
- exclusive sources
- signal workflows

### Use less of

- truth discovery
- institutional bedrock
- generic finance abstractions
- over-processed pitch-deck phrasing
- “problem / outcome / bottleneck” framing
- unsupported precision claims
- named data partners unless intentionally approved

### Tone

The desired tone is:

- sober
- clear
- technical
- restrained
- not fluffy
- not “AI slop”
- not investor-deck language

Shorter, harder copy consistently tested better during this session.

## 8. Website Structure We Landed On

The homepage story currently follows this order:

1. Hero
2. Market quality / thin markets section
3. What we do
4. Better inputs / unfair advantage
5. Execution engine proof
6. Selective capital partners
7. Final CTA

### Fold-by-fold intent

#### Hero

State what EmeTruth is in the fewest words possible.

#### Market quality fold

Show that thin markets lead directly to trader loss and retention problems.

#### What we do fold

Show the actual liquidity program:

- ongoing market making
- launch seeding
- market design
- venue collaboration

#### Better Inputs fold

Make the alternative-data edge explicit.

This fold should carry the unfair advantage story.

#### Engine In Use fold

Prove that the engine is live and real.

This fold should carry proof, not theory.

#### Selective Capital fold

Keep LP access present but secondary.

## 9. Website Copy Decisions

These are the main copy decisions we converged on.

### Keep

- `Liquidity Layer that scales Prediction Markets.`
- `EmeTruth makes prediction markets liquid and efficient.`
- `If markets are thin, traders leave.`
- `Better Inputs / Better Markets`
- `Engine In Use`
- `Selective Capital Partners`

### Avoid

- `Built, Not Pitched`
- `Market Making For This Category`
- `The Problem / The Outcome`
- public Wintermute/GSR reference language
- explicit deal-structure copy on the website

## 10. Visual / Design Decisions

The page started to drift into multiple card systems.

We normalized the visual language so the major folds now share:

- similar card radius
- similar border treatment
- similar glass/surface behavior
- similar hover behavior
- gold accent bars
- more consistent CTA shape and finish

One intentional exception remains:

- the program cards still use icons

That is acceptable and was explicitly kept.

## 11. Open Cautions

These should carry into any sales pitch or public narrative.

- Do not overclaim on measurable performance unless it can be backed up.
- Keep data-provider references generic unless there is a reason to name them.
- Keep the LP story secondary in public-facing material.
- Keep the proof story centered on real usage and the engine.
- Keep the unfair-advantage story centered on alternative data plus workflows, not generic “we are better traders.”

If the next task is to generate talking points or a sales pitch, the strongest anchors are:

### Who we are

EmeTruth is liquidity infrastructure for prediction markets.

### What problem we solve

Prediction markets fail when market quality is weak. Thin markets drive users away.

### Why believe it
