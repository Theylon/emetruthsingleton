# EmeTruth Sales Pitch For Prediction Market Teams

This document turns the positioning work into a usable sales narrative for founders, heads of markets, and platform operators at prediction market venues.

The goal is to help EmeTruth explain:

- what it is
- what problem it solves
- why it is different
- why a venue should care now
- what the next step should be

## 1. Short Version

### One-line pitch

EmeTruth makes prediction markets liquid and efficient.

### Slightly longer version

EmeTruth is liquidity infrastructure for prediction markets. We help venues improve market quality through specialist market making, better pricing inputs, and execution built specifically for this category.

### Operator-first version

If markets are thin, traders leave. We help prediction market teams fix that with a full liquidity program built for launches, live markets, and event-driven repricing.

## 2. Core Narrative

### The setup

Prediction markets do not fail because people do not care.

They fail because the trading experience breaks:

- books are thin
- spreads are wide
- new markets open weakly
- users try the product once and do not come back

For a prediction market team, liquidity is not a nice-to-have.

It is product quality.

### The problem

If markets are thin, traders leave.

That hurts:

- retention
- confidence
- volume
- open interest
- the quality of new market launches

### The solution

EmeTruth runs a specialist liquidity program for prediction markets.

That includes:

- ongoing market making
- launch seeding
- market design input
- venue collaboration when infrastructure needs improvement

### Why EmeTruth

Most market makers bring capital and execution.

EmeTruth’s edge is that it also brings:

- access to alternative and exclusive data sources
- workflows that turn raw signals into pricing inputs
- an execution engine already in use in prediction markets

That combination matters because prediction markets are not generic crypto order books.

They are event-driven markets where openings, repricing, and inventory discipline matter much more.

## 5. Discovery Questions For A Venue

Use these early in the conversation.

### Market quality

- Where do you feel the liquidity problem most today?
- Are the weakest points live books, new launches, or specific categories of markets?
- What do traders complain about most?

### Retention

- Do you think liquidity quality is hurting repeat usage?
- Which user behaviors suggest churn caused by weak markets?

### Launch quality

- What usually happens when you open a new market?
- Which launches have felt weaker than they should?

### Infrastructure

- How strong is your current API and execution setup?
- Are there infrastructure issues that make good market making harder than it should be?

### Current setup

- Are you already working with any market makers?
- If yes, what is missing from that relationship?

## 6. Objection Handling

### “We already have liquidity.”

That may be true in parts of the book, but the real question is whether the market consistently feels tradable, especially during launches and repricing. We focus on market quality, not just the appearance of activity.

### “Another market maker can do this.”

Some can provide capital and quoting. What is harder to replicate is the combination of category focus, alternative data inputs, structured pricing workflows, and an execution engine already in use in prediction markets.

### “We are too early for this.”

That is usually the moment when liquidity matters most. Weak early market quality is one of the fastest ways to lose momentum and train users not to come back.

### “Why not wait until volume grows?”

Because weak market quality is often one reason volume does not grow in the first place.

### “What do you actually change for us?”

Better launches, better market quality, tighter execution, and a better trader experience that supports retention.

## 7. What To Emphasize In A Meeting

### Lead with

- market quality
- retention
- thin markets as a user problem
- prediction-market specialization
- alternative data and pricing workflows
- the live execution engine

### Do not lead with

- LPs
- fund language
- rebates or pricing structure
- broad crypto comparisons
- vague “institutional” branding language
